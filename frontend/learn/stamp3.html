Trace This Seal

Dynasty Yuan
Type Personal authentication seal
Stamped Year 1295, shortly after the painting’s completion
Current Location National Palace Museum, Taipei, China

Historical Background: Identity and Origin of the Seal

The seal reading “Zhao Shi Zi’ang” (赵氏子昂)—literally “Zhao clan, [courtesy name] Zi’ang”—is a personal authentication seal belonging to Zhao Mengfu (b.1254–d.1322). Zhao Mengfu, courtesy name Zi’ang, was a distinguished Chinese calligrapher, painter, and poet of the late Southern Song and early Yuan dynasty. He was the eleventh-generation descendant of Emperor Taizu of the Song dynasty, and a direct heir of Prince Qin, Zhao Defang. The seal format—combining the clan name “Zhao” with the courtesy name “Zi’ang”—follows the classical surname-plus-courtesy-name convention popular among Song and Yuan literati. Autumn Colors on the Qiao and Hua Mountains was painted in the first year of the Yuanzhen era (1295), after Zhao Mengfu returned south from his post in Jinan, as a gift for his close friend Zhou Mi, depicting the landscape of Zhou’s ancestral homeland. This seal is stamped directly on the scroll and constitutes the most immediate physical marker of authorship on the work. Among the many seals documented on this scroll, this seal appears as the very first one in the sequence; it is the earliest impression on the entire painting, with all subsequent seals belonging to later collectors and connoisseurs.
